PRIVACY POLICY – SMCC

This Privacy Policy (together with our Terms and Conditions and any other documents referred to in it) is compliant with GDPR and shows how and why we process personal data. It sets out the basis on which any personal data we collect from you, or that you provide to us, will be processed by us.

The privacy of our members is essential to us, and we have designed this policy to make it as simple as possible for you to stay informed and confident about how your information is processed. Please read the following carefully to understand our views and practices regarding your personal data and how we will treat it. This policy will be updated regularly.

1. WHO ARE “WE”?

The Swedish-Macedonian Chamber of Commerce (SMCC), registered in Skopje and Stockholm under company numbers 7871015 in Skopje and 502100-0327 in Stockholm, is a non-profit organization dedicated to promoting trade, investment, and cooperation between the Kingdom of Sweden and the Republic of Macedonia. The SMCC is a membership-based organization that connects businesses, entrepreneurs, and institutions from both countries. Our activities include organizing business events, delegations, roundtables, and seminars, as well as providing introductions, consulting, and promotional opportunities for our members.

If you have any questions about this privacy policy or how we process your personal data, please contact us at: info@smcc.mk or addressed to Swedish-Macedonian Chamber of Commerce, Zvanska 5, 1000 Skopje, North Macedonia.

2. WHAT PERSONAL DATA DO WE COLLECT?

The SMCC primarily collects information directly from its member company representatives or from others who have expressed interest in the organisation. The information collected is limited to contact details such as names, telephone numbers, emails, and addresses. The personal data is collected in order for us to provide the services that our members and partners expect and to make communications as effective and simple as possible.

We collect personal data in connection with activities such as membership registration requests, signing up for the newsletter, or participating in our events and delegations. The information is most often collected directly from the data subject themselves through email, phone, or face-to-face meetings. In some cases, data may be collected via referral from colleagues or from publicly available sources such as company websites. We do not collect any sensitive personal data.

3. HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION

Our processing is used for direct communication and to uphold a relevant and appropriate relationship with our members, partners, and potential members according to what you would reasonably expect. We use your information to provide services that you have at some point expressed interest in. These services include, but are not limited to, event invitations, newsletters, special offers from other members, introductions to possible collaborators, and opportunities for exposure within the network through sponsorships or partnerships.

Most of the time, the data is used to service our memberships and to inform our members about how they can participate in or contribute to upcoming activities. We may contact you by email or by phone, and you may simply request to stop receiving such information at any time. We use a third-party provider to deliver our newsletters and gather statistics about the number of email openings and clicks. We do not use any obtained data for profiling.

We are very aware of the importance of protecting the data of our members, and we never share it with any third parties except for services we have contractual relationships with that do not use the data for their own purposes, such as email marketing providers or CRM systems. On some occasions, we may provide your name and company to event partners who are listed when signing up for that particular event. This is done only to ensure that the registration process is handled smoothly and securely. We also use cookies on our website (covered in section six).

4. WHY DO WE COLLECT YOUR INFORMATION?

The reason we collect your information is that you decided to become a member or requested information from us in another matter. Thus, we keep your data in order to perform services in which you have expressed an interest. These could include event invitations, cooperation offers, and partnership opportunities. Apart from members, we are regularly contacted by people and institutions with an interest in Swedish-Macedonian trade and bilateral cooperation.

5. OUR LEGITIMATE INTERESTS

The SMCC has chosen Legitimate Interest as the lawful basis for processing personal data. The reason we chose legitimate interest is that our data processing has minimal privacy impact and is in line with people’s reasonable expectations when signing up for a membership or contacting us for cooperation. Also, since there is no other reasonable or less intrusive way to achieve the same result, legitimate interest is the most appropriate lawful basis for processing our data. Furthermore, our Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA) shows that our data processing is purposeful, necessary, and balanced with the individual’s interests. We consider this processing necessary and not inflicting any harm to individual rights and freedoms. Contact us if you wish to receive a copy of our LIA.

The legitimate interests that we have identified are as follows: Direct communication and marketing – we send regular emails regarding upcoming events and collaboration opportunities. Relevant and appropriate relationship – as expected of a membership organisation, we know and communicate with most of our members regularly and treat them as clients. Thus, we keep data in order to secure this relationship, such as contact information and, where relevant, dietary requirements for event participation. Reasonable expectations – when signing up for a membership or participating in our events, it includes communication and informational updates from the Chamber. This is reasonable to expect from individuals who have willingly signed up for these activities. Reasonable expectations: When signing up for a membership, it includes a complimentary subscription to our magazine and thus we process addresses when distributing the magazine. This is reasonable to expect for the individual as they gladly signed up to get the magazine.

6. SECURITY

We are taking appropriate measures to prevent and minimise risks of unauthorised access to personal information. However, the Internet is not a totally secure medium, and we shall not be responsible for any unauthorised use, distribution, damage, or destruction of personal data. Where we have given the user a password or login that enables access to certain restricted parts of our site, you are responsible for doing everything you reasonably can to keep these details secret.

Unfortunately, the transmission of information over the Internet or public communications networks can never be completely secure. We will take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect the personal data that you submit to us against unauthorised or unlawful access or loss, destruction, or damage, although we cannot guarantee 100% security of data that you provide to us online. We will take reasonable precautions to prevent the loss, misuse, or alteration of your personal information. We will store all the personal information you provide on our secure (password- and firewall-protected) servers. All electronic transactions entered via the website will be protected by encryption technology.

7. COOKIES & IP ADDRESSES

Cookies are small text files of information that are stored on the visitor’s computer in order for the site to remember who they are. Cookies can only be stored if consent is given, and you can read more about cookies on the link (http://www.allaboutcookies.org/). The SMCC uses cookies on its website to identify the user. They are only used in very limited contexts, such as member login.

We also use Google Analytics to track the activity on our website, but it is not used for profiling or tracking specific individuals. It is used to understand which sections of our website are most popular and to improve our content and user experience. If you do not want your usage to be tracked by Google Analytics, please follow its opt-out instructions through the link (https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout).

We may collect information about your computer, including, where available, your IP address, operating system, and browser type, for system administration and to report aggregate information to our partners. This is statistical data about our users’ browsing actions and patterns, and does not identify any individual.

8. YOUR RIGHTS

According to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the right to receive a copy of the personal information that we hold about you (right of access) and the right to be informed about how we use your personal information (right to be informed). You also have the right to correct any information that is not accurate (right of rectification), the right to ask us to stop using your information (right to restrict processing), and the right to have your personal information removed from our records (right to be forgotten). You also have the right to object to processing for marketing purposes (right to object).

If you would like to know more about your rights under the GDPR, please visit the website of the relevant data protection authority in your country. Please note that although our site may contain links to websites of our partners, we cannot accept liability for their privacy policies and encourage you to read them before submitting any personal data.

9. HOW TO OPT OUT OF RECEIVING MESSAGES FROM THE SMCC

To stop receiving our newsletters, you can simply click the unsubscribe button at the bottom of the email. To stop receiving direct contact (postal or telephone), please contact us with information about what you wish to opt out of. If you have an active membership, we advise you not to unsubscribe from all communications, as that would mean missing important information, updates, and opportunities related to your membership.
